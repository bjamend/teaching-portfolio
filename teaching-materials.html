Lecture Materials

I have tutored, led supplemental instruction sessions, and given guest lectures over the course of my undergraduate and graduate education. Included here are representative materials from a single guest lecture, including a recording of the lecture itself, the slide deck including clicker questions, and materials for one of the active learning exercises. This lecture was for the course "Physics for Everyone" at Clemson University, a gen-ed class for non-science majors. Student and observing faculty feedback for this lecture can be found on the 'Lecture Evaluations' page.

And below are the materials for the selected active learning exercise. In this activity, students compare printed element spectra with the solar spectrum to identify which elements make up the Sun. They should conclude that Argon and Neon are absent (although these elements exist in trace amounts in the Sun, the mock solar spectrum used here includes only Fraunhofer lines). The most observant students may also notice that some lines in the solar spectrum do not match any of the provided element spectra. These unexplained lines correspond to Helium.
